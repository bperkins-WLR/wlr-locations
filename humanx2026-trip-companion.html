🌉 San Francisco Guide
You've got Sunday afternoon + all of Monday before HumanX kicks off Tuesday.
Here's the best of SF for you and Brad — curated for your interests.
⚡ Arrive-Day Essentials
🚇Take BART from SFO → Montgomery St station — ~30 min, ~$10/person. Fastest way downtown. Then a short Uber to the hotel.
🧳Check-in is typically 4:00 PM at the Marriott Marquis. Drop bags at the bell desk and head out — don't wait around.
🕐You land 9:16 AM SF time — but your body thinks it's 12:16 PM EDT. You'll feel wide awake. Use it.
🌡️April SF weather: 55–65°F, often foggy in the morning, clearer by afternoon. Bring a layer — always.
🚗Uber/Lyft work great everywhere. Parking is a headache. Don't rent a car unless you're heading out of the city.
SUN · APR 5 Arrival Day — Hit the Ground Running
🏛️
Ferry Building Marketplace
First Stop
SF's iconic food hall right on the waterfront. Grab lunch from the best local vendors — Hog Island Oysters, Acme Bread, Blue Bottle Coffee. The Sunday Farmer's Market runs until 2 PM so you might catch the tail end.
📍 1 Ferry Building · ~10 min Uber from hotel · Free to enter
🗺️ Directions
🌊
Embarcadero Waterfront Walk
Recommended
Walk from the Ferry Building north along the waterfront. Bay Bridge views, street performers, the piers. Stretch your legs after 6 hours on a plane — this is perfect. Connects naturally to Fisherman's Wharf.
🚶 ~2 mile walk north along the water · Free
🗺️ Directions
🦭
Fisherman's Wharf & Pier 39
Classic SF
Touristy? Yes. Worth it? Also yes. The sea lions at Pier 39 are genuinely wild. Grab clam chowder in a sourdough bread bowl from Boudin Bakery — it's the iconic SF meal. Don't skip it.
📍 Pier 39 · 20 min Uber from hotel · Free (food extra)
🗺️ Directions
🏝️
Alcatraz Island
Book Ahead!
One of the most unique experiences in America. The audio tour narrated by former guards and inmates is genuinely fascinating. Day tours often sell out weeks in advance — book at alcatrazcruises.com NOW if you want to go.
⚠️ Book online ASAP · Departs Pier 33 · ~$45/person · 2.5 hrs
🗺️ Directions to Pier 33 🎟️ Book Alcatraz Tickets
🌁
Golden Gate Bridge
Bucket List
Walk across the bridge — it's completely free and unforgettable. Or Uber to the Marin Headlands side for the iconic photo looking back at the bridge with the city behind it. Best photos: late afternoon golden hour.
📍 ~25 min Uber from hotel · Free to walk · 1.7 miles each way
🗺️ Directions
🚎
Cable Car Ride
SF Icon
The Powell-Hyde line is the most scenic — ride it up over Nob Hill, past Lombard Street, all the way to Ghirardelli Square near the waterfront. Standing on the outside rail with the city dropping away is a genuine thrill.
📍 Powell & Market St · $8/ride each way · Lines can be long on weekends
🗺️ Directions to Boarding Stop
🪄
Lombard Street
Worth Seeing
The famously "crookedest street in the world" — 8 hairpin turns down a steep one-block section of Lombard. Walk down it (not up). Great photo spot from the bottom looking up. Easy to combine with Fisherman's Wharf.
📍 Lombard St at Hyde St · Free · 5 min Uber from Wharf
🗺️ Directions
🍜
Chinatown + North Beach
Dinner Zone
Two great neighborhoods back-to-back. SF's Chinatown is the oldest in North America — walk through Grant Ave, grab dim sum or pork buns. Then head uphill into North Beach (Little Italy) for dinner. City Lights Bookstore is a legendary Beat Generation landmark worth a quick stop.
📍 15 min Uber from hotel · Free to explore
🗺️ Directions to Chinatown
🍽️ Sunday Dinner Picks
Cotogna
North Beach · Rustic Italian wood-fired cooking. One of SF's best. Make a reservation. ~$60/person
Hog Island Oyster Co.
Ferry Building · Outstanding fresh oysters and seafood in a waterfront setting. ~$50/person
Foreign Cinema
Mission District · Dinner served in an outdoor courtyard while old films project on the wall. Unique SF experience. ~$65/person
MON · APR 6 Full Free Day — Go Deep
🔬
California Academy of Sciences
🚀 Brad's Pick
World-class natural history museum with a living rainforest, coral reef aquarium, and the Morrison Planetarium. A NASA guy in a state-of-the-art planetarium — this one writes itself. Allow 3+ hours.
📍 Golden Gate Park · $45/person · Open Mon–Sun · Book tickets online
🗺️ Directions 🎟️ Buy Tickets
🎨
de Young Museum
Brice's Pick
SF's premier art museum right in Golden Gate Park — American art, textiles, and major rotating exhibitions. The tower observation deck is free and has stunning 360° views of the city and bay. This is right next door to the Academy of Sciences, so hit both in one morning.
📍 Golden Gate Park · $30/person · Closed Mon — plan for afternoon
🗺️ Directions 🎟️ Buy Tickets
⛵
Ferry to Sausalito
Top Pick
30-minute Golden Gate Ferry ride from the Ferry Building with stunning views of Alcatraz and the bridge. Sausalito is a charming waterfront village with great galleries, restaurants, and views back at SF. Walk around, grab lunch, ferry back. This is legitimately one of the best things you can do in SF.
📍 Departs Ferry Building · ~$15 each way · Ferries run hourly
🗺️ Directions to Ferry 🚢 Ferry Schedule
🌲
Muir Woods National Monument
Don't Miss
Ancient coastal redwoods — some over 1,000 years old — just 30 minutes north of the city. Walking among trees that were saplings when the Magna Carta was signed is a genuinely humbling experience. Must book a parking reservation or take a shuttle in advance at recreation.gov.
📍 Mill Valley · $16/person entry · 45 min drive · Book parking ahead
🗺️ Directions 🌲 Book Parking
🏛️
SFMOMA
For Brice
SF Museum of Modern Art — one of the largest modern art museums in the US, and it's literally a 7-minute walk from your hotel. World-class photography, design, and media art collections. If you have a couple free hours, this is effortless.
📍 151 3rd St · 7 min walk from hotel · $30/person · Closed Tues
🗺️ Directions 🎟️ Buy Tickets
🌅
Lands End + Sutro Baths
Hidden Gem
The ruins of a massive Victorian public bathhouse right on the Pacific coast — dramatic, photogenic, and totally free. The coastal trail at Lands End gives you arguably the best views of the Golden Gate Bridge from land. Eerily beautiful at any time of day.
📍 Outer Richmond · 25 min Uber · Free · Ocean Beach nearby
🗺️ Directions
🏡
Painted Ladies + Alamo Square
Iconic Photo
The famous row of colorful Victorian homes with the SF skyline behind them. If you've seen any movie or TV show set in SF, you've seen this shot. Easy stop, great photo, worth the Uber. Combine with a walk through Hayes Valley for coffee and boutique shopping.
📍 Alamo Square Park · 15 min Uber · Free
🗺️ Directions
🌆
Twin Peaks at Sunset
Night Cap
Drive or Uber to the top of Twin Peaks for a 360° panoramic view of the entire city, bay, and both bridges. It's stunning during the day but transcendent at sunset and after dark when the city lights up. Perfect way to end your last free evening before the conference.
📍 Twin Peaks Blvd · 20 min Uber · Free · Go at sunset
🗺️ Directions
🍽️ Monday Pre-Conference Dinner
State Bird Provisions
Divisadero · Dim sum–style small plates, one of SF's most celebrated restaurants. Reserve well in advance. ~$80/person
Tartine Manufactory
Mission District · The legendary Tartine bakery's full restaurant. Stunning breads, pastas, wood-fired dishes. ~$55/person
Zuni Café
Hayes Valley · SF institution since 1979. The roasted chicken for two (45 min wait) is legendary. Perfect pre-conference splurge. ~$70/person
💡 Pro Tips for Your Trip
🎟️Book Alcatraz now at alcatrazcruises.com — tickets sell out weeks ahead, especially on weekends.
🌲Muir Woods parking must be reserved at recreation.gov — you cannot just show up. Shuttle from Sausalito is an easier option.
🍽️Make dinner reservations now — SF's best spots (Cotogna, State Bird, Zuni) book up fast, especially on weekends.
🎨de Young Museum is closed Mondays — go Sunday afternoon if you want to catch it.
🚇BART tip: Buy a Clipper card at the SFO station (add $20–30). Works on BART, Muni buses, and cable cars.
📸Best photo spots: Marin Headlands (GG Bridge), Lands End trail, Coit Tower, and the Ferry Building at dusk.
🏨The Moscone Center (conference venue) is a 3-minute walk from your hotel. Scout it out Monday evening so you're not rushing Tuesday.
